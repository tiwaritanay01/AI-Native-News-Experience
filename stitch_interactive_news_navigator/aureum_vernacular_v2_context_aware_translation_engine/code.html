Hawkish Pivot: Fed Signals Long-Term Yield Hold

The Federal Reserve's latest minutes suggest a hawkish pivot that has caught institutional investors flat-footed. While the market was pricing in a rate cut by Q3, the central bank appears determined to stay the course until inflation reaches the magic 2% target.

This double-edged sword presents a challenge for emerging markets. As the dollar strengthens, capital flight becomes a zero-sum game for developing economies struggling with sovereign debt.

Analysts suggest that the current bull trap in tech stocks might be the final gasp before a broader market correction. The smart money is already rotating into defensive utilities and energy plays.
